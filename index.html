Customer Churn Analysis & Retention Strategy

A full BRD-to-dashboard build for a bank customer base — walked through stage by stage below.

01 Requirements 02 Warehouse 03 Cleaning 04 Enrichment 05 Modeling 06 Reporting 07 Dashboard 08 Insights
01
Business Requirements

Starting with the BRD, not the data

Before touching a single row of data, the project opened with a Business Requirement Document defining the problem: identify the key factors driving customer churn at a bank and turn them into a retention strategy management could act on.

Objectives:

  • Identify characteristics of customers likely to churn
  • Quantify churn rate and segment customers by risk level
  • Develop actionable insights for a retention strategy
  • Enable management to monitor churn KPIs through Power BI
  • Establish a repeatable pipeline from ingestion to reporting

Guiding business questions (refined during EDA): does age, tenure, or balance influence churn? Are inactive customers or non-cardholders more likely to leave? Which geography or gender shows higher churn? How does credit score correlate with churn?

Data Cleaning & Transformation

From RAW to something modelable

Data was pulled from Snowflake's RAW layer into Python for cleaning and validation before being written back to CLEAN — checking for missing values, mapping coded fields (geography, gender) to readable labels, and validating date fields against tenure.

# Connect using environment variables — never hardcoded credentials
import os, pandas as pd
from snowflake.connector import connect

details = {
  'user': os.environ['SNOWFLAKE_USER'],
  'password': os.environ['SNOWFLAKE_PASSWORD'],
  'account': os.environ['SNOWFLAKE_ACCOUNT'],
  'warehouse': 'BANK_WH', 'database': 'BANK_ANALYTICS_DB', 'schema': 'RAW'
}

conn = connect(**details)
df = pd.read_sql('SELECT * FROM BANK_CHURN', conn)

Credentials load from environment variables rather than being committed to source — a small detail, but one that matters the moment a repo goes public.

05
Data Modeling

A proper star schema, not one flat table

The analytics layer is modeled as a star schema — a central FACT_CUSTOMER_CHURN_v1 table surrounded by six dimension tables (geography, gender, exit status, active status, credit card ownership, customer info). This is what makes the Power BI report fast and lets DAX measures filter cleanly across any dimension.

07
Dashboard

The Executive Overview

The landing page for management: total customers, churn rate against target, average tenure, and a country-level breakdown — with drill-through into deeper demographic pages.

Executive Overview — Power BI
Overall churn

2,037 of 10,000 customers exited — a 20.37% churn rate, above the 15% target across all observed years (2016–2019).

Geography

Germany churns hardest at 32.4%, vs. Spain 16.7% and France 16.2% — no country fell below the 16.5% overall average.

Activity status

Inactive members churn roughly 2–3× more than active ones — the clearest, most consistent signal in the dataset.

Credit card ownership

Non-cardholders churn more than cardholders, pointing to a possible cross-sell opportunity.

08
Insights & Recommendations

What the data actually supports

Churn concentrates among mid-age customers (25–45) who are inactive and don't hold a credit card. Geography and credit score show weaker, less consistent effects and shouldn't be treated as primary drivers without further testing — engagement and product adoption are the clearest, most defensible levers here.

Recommended next steps: activate dormant customers, incentivize credit card adoption, and personalize retention campaigns for high-risk profiles — ideally backed by a proper classification model rather than manually chosen risk weights.

Honest disclosure

The source dataset has no churn-reason field. The "churn drivers" shown on the dashboard (better rewards elsewhere, relocation, service cost, etc.) are simulated sample data built to demonstrate decomposition-tree and drill-through features — not derived from real customer records. A production version would need a genuine exit-survey table joined in via a new dimension. Flagging this here rather than presenting it as real is part of the point.
